What the realm does with aetherworks spoil. 0–33 disperse (spread thin by wind and terrain); 34–66 concentrate (all of it onto one sacrifice zone); 67–100 treat (clean it up where coin and works allow).

THE SEAT
How likely the capital is to meet the realm's first crisis with relief instead of force. The dice still roll; this only weights them. 45 = the old world.
The order axis, from open ground to police state. High order holds down predation, smuggling, and revolt. It also holds down mobility, new entry, and the appetite to risk capital. Safety and stagnation come from the same place. 50 = the old world.
Foreign trade and diplomacy exposure, from sealed quays to open coast. It scales the sea leg of trade, so a trade world grows a second pole at the coast. At 0 it seals the harbors entirely: no ports, no Dominion. 100 = open (the old world).
Seal the quays and you lose sea trade, port tariffs, and the door the Dominion comes through. Isolation is a policy, and it costs something.
TIME
Run the world forward: ore depletes, wealth compounds, people migrate, the grid chases the winners, the capital decrees, towns rise. 0 = the founding snapshot.

WHAT THIS WORLD SHOWS, ALL COMPUTED FROM ITS OWN EXPORTED COLUMNS

Every map this makes is an argument about inequality: who gets served and who sits in darkness, who breathes the poison and who ships it downstream, who holds the gates and who pays the tariff. None of it is painted. It is all emergent from blind geology, a cost-gated grid, and factions that seize, tax, and go to war. The fantasy is only the presentation. The real content is the attribute table. Seeded, reproducible, QGIS-ready.
